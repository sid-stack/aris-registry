## Competitors & “do we need SOC 2 / ISO now?”

**Reality:**  
- **SOC 2 Type II** (or ISO 27001) is often a **checkbox for enterprise** and **some** federal-facing SaaS—but many **small GovCon** buyers buy on **trust, referrals, and pragmatic security Q&A**, not a badge on day one.  
- **If your direct competitors show SOC 2** and you sell to the **same mid-market + enterprise**, you will **eventually** need it—or a **credible alternative** (HIPAA-style BAAs rarely apply; focus on **data flow, retention, subprocessors, encryption, access logs**).  
- **MoSCoW placement:**  
  - **Must:** Accurate security story + contracts that match reality.  
  - **Should:** SOC 2 readiness (policies, logging, access) *before* full audit if pipeline asks repeatedly.  
  - **Could:** ISO if a prime demands it.  
  - **Won’t now:** Full enterprise GRC theater without a paying champion to fund it.

**Action:** Track **# of lost deals citing “no SOC 2”**; if >25% of qualified demos, pull **S4** into **Must** with a date.

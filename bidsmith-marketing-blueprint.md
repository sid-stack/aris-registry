# BidSmith Marketing Blueprint

### Position: The GovCon tool for firms Sweetspot priced out.

---

## The One-Line Strategy

**Sweetspot is building for Oshkosh. We're building for the 10,000 firms below them.**

Every dollar Sweetspot raises pushes them upmarket. Every enterprise logo they land (Groq, Vannevar, Flexport) is a signal to their VC that they should raise prices and add salespeople. That's our opening. We go the other direction — instant, self-serve, priced for a 3-person BD team.

---

## Positioning Statement

**For:** Small GovCon firms (20–200 people) with 1–5 person BD teams
**Who:** Are manually qualifying RFPs, building compliance matrices in Word/Excel, and losing bids because they're slow
**BidSmith is:** The only GovCon bid tool you can use without a demo, a consultant, or a $500/mo minimum
**Unlike:** Sweetspot and GovWin, which require onboarding, sales calls, and enterprise budgets
**We deliver:** Bid/no-bid decision + compliance matrix in 60 seconds — free to try, no credit card

## Target Personas (ranked by urgency)

### Persona 1 — The Overwhelmed BD Manager (highest priority)
- Title: Business Development Manager / Director of Capture
- Company: 30–150 person IT services or consulting GovCon firm
- Pain: Reviewing 20+ SAM.gov opportunities per week manually, missing deadlines, low win rate
- Budget authority: Yes or direct line to CEO
- Where to reach: LinkedIn, APMP community, GovCon forums
- Hook: Time savings + win rate

### Persona 2 — The Founder/CEO Doing BD Themselves
- Title: CEO / President / Managing Partner
- Company: 15–50 person firm, no dedicated BD staff yet
- Pain: Spending 20+ hrs/week on bids personally, needs to delegate but can't afford a Proposal Manager
- Budget authority: Full
- Where to reach: LinkedIn, GovCon Twitter/X, email
- Hook: "Do in 60 seconds what takes your team a day"

### Persona 3 — The Proposal Manager Trying to Scale
- Title: Proposal Manager / Proposal Coordinator
- Company: 50–200 person firm
- Pain: Volume is increasing, tools are stuck in 2010 (Shipley binders, Word docs)
- Budget authority: Needs sign-off but champions the tool
- Where to reach: APMP, LinkedIn, Slack communities
- Hook: Compliance matrix automation + XLSX export

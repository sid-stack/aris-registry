**OPPORTUNITY SEGMENT THIS WEEK**

**Target:** Small business IT services at Civilian agencies (GSA, HHS, DHS)

GSA is running 12 active task order competitions this quarter, primarily in IT modernization and cybersecurity. Average award value: $8–22M. Small business set-asides make up 60%+ of the pipeline.

Signal: GSA Schedule holders with CIO-SP3 or Alliant 2 experience are seeing above-average award rates this cycle. If you're positioned on these vehicles, prioritize your pursuit list for Q3 civilian competitions.

→ [Search active GSA solicitations on BidSmith](https://bidsmith.pro/search)

---

**COMPLIANCE INSIGHT**

**Watch: FAR 52.215-1 Clarifications vs. Discussions**

This week's compliance flag: several contractors have been caught by the line between government clarifications and discussions.

If you receive a clarification request post-submission, you can only clarify — not correct or improve your proposal. Discussions (when opened) allow substantive changes.

The risk: responding to a clarification as if it were a discussion can be used to justify a non-award finding.

Rule: If the government asks for clarification, answer only what's asked. Don't volunteer improvements.

---

**PROCESS TIP**

**Set a 72-hour debrief request rule.**

Within 72 hours of any contract loss notification, your BD lead sends the debrief request. No exceptions.

The government is required to respond. The debrief tells you your technical score, your price position, and the specific weaknesses evaluators found.

Most contractors skip the debrief because losing is uncomfortable. The contractors who request every debrief improve their win rate 15–20% over 12 months.

One template, 72-hour rule, automatic.

---

**MARKET PULSE**

- Defense health IT: DHA is releasing 4 new task order competitions in Q3 under the MHS GENESIS vehicle. Source: DHA procurement forecast.
- DOGE effect: OMB has issued informal guidance to reduce sole-source awards over $500K. Expect more competitive solicitations at agencies currently running heavy sole-source pipelines.
- SBIR Phase II: DOD Phase II selections are running 3–4 weeks slower than usual. If you're in the queue, expected delays don't indicate deselection.

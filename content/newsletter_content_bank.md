# The Bid Brief — content bank (2× / week)

Use one row per send: pick **one** segment + **one** compliance note + **one** workflow tip. Keep under 500 words. CTA: [Start free audit](https://www.bidsmith.pro/dashboard) (limits apply).

## Subject line stash (rotate)

1. “Section L changed again — did your pipeline notice?”
2. “One set-aside pattern worth 30 minutes this week”
3. “Bid/no-bid: when ‘we can technically bid’ is the wrong yes”
4. “FAR clause that quietly eats small primes”
5. “SAM.gov hygiene: 3 checks before you staff capture”
6. “Past performance language that reads tighter than you think”
7. “Best value vs LPTA — pick your week, not your ego”
8. “Amendment stack: when to re-run your first pass”
9. “CMMC / cyber clauses: gate early, not at Red Team”
10. “Agency X is busy — here is the signal, not the noise”
11. “Subcontracting plan: when it becomes a disqualifier”
12. “Your competitor’s win theme is not your strategy”

*(Replace “Agency X” with a real agency or vehicle when you have a defensible public signal.)*

## Issue skeleton (paste into Beehiiv)

**Opening (2–3 sentences)** — one timely observation (policy, volume, or a pattern you saw in audits / Reddit / SAM).

**Segment focus** — “This week watch:” + NAICS band, vehicle, or agency + *why it matters now*.

**Compliance / evaluation note** — one clause type or evaluation shape + “what to grep for in Section L/M.”

**Workflow** — one habit (e.g. re-audit after amendment N, matrix row ownership, BD gate checklist).

**Sign-off** — 1 line founder voice + single CTA.

## Compliance / trust

- No “guaranteed win” language — **decision support** and **time saved** only.
- If you cite dollars or contract counts, link a **public** source (SAM, agency page, USASpending).

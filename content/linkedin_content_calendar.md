### Post 11 — Wednesday (Educational)
> Most people don't know that SAM.gov publishes Sources Sought notices 30–90 days before the final RFP.
>
> These notices mean: the agency is planning a procurement and wants to know who's out there.
>
> Responding to a Sources Sought is free and positions you before the competition starts. Agencies track respondents.
>
> If your team only monitors final RFPs, you're starting every race in second place.

# BidSmith Traffic Generation Plan
**Goal:** Drive demo bookings & signups to bidsmith.pro
**Constraint:** 2–5 hrs/week
**Date:** March 31, 2026

---

## North Star Metric
**Qualified demo requests per week** — not raw traffic, not impressions.

Every channel is evaluated by one question: *Does this put a government contractor in front of bidsmith.pro with intent to solve a problem?*

---

## Tier 1 — Highest ROI (Do These First)

### 1. Cold Email → Site (Already Running — Optimize It)
**Time:** 30 min/week | **Impact:** Immediate

Your outbound is live. The gap right now is the email is the conversation, not the site. Fix that.

**Actions:**
- Every cold email should have ONE link: a Loom walkthrough OR a calendar link hosted on bidsmith.pro (e.g., bidsmith.pro/demo)
- Add a P.S. line to every cold email: *"P.S. See how it works in 90 seconds → bidsmith.pro"*
- Follow-up emails (Day 3, Day 7) should reference the site explicitly: *"Sent you a note earlier — here's a quick look at what BidSmith does: bidsmith.pro"*

**Why:** You're already getting eyeballs. Route them to the site. Every reply chain is a warm visitor.

---

### 2. LinkedIn Organic (Founder-Led Content)
**Time:** 1–2 hrs/week | **Impact:** Compounds over 30–60 days

Government contractors follow founders who speak their language. You don't need followers — you need the right 50 people to see you every week.

**Posting Cadence:** 3x per week

**Content Pillars:**

| Pillar | Format | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Pain Point | Short post (3–5 lines) | "Most proposal teams spend 60% of bid time on formatting. That's not strategy, that's overhead." |
| Signal-Based Insight | Data post | "SAM.gov had 4,200+ new solicitations last week. How many did you actually evaluate?" |
| Product Proof | Before/After | "We ran a bid/no-bid analysis in 45 seconds on a 200-page RFP. Here's what we found." |
| Social Proof (future) | Quote / DM screenshot | When you get replies, post them (anonymized) |

**Rules:**
- No hashtag spam
- End every post with a soft CTA: *"If this is familiar, check out what we built → bidsmith.pro"*
- Comment on posts from GovCon BD leads before posting your own (warms the algorithm)

---

### 3. Blog SEO — Target Buyer-Intent Keywords
**Time:** 1 hr/week | **Impact:** 60–90 day compounding

You have a blog. Most competitors don't write content that ranks for the exact phrases your ICP Googles.

**Target Keywords (High Intent, Low Competition):**

| Keyword | Monthly Searches | Intent |
|---------|-----------------|--------|
| "how to win government RFPs" | Medium | Research |
| "RFP win rate improvement" | Low | High buyer intent |
| "government bid management software" | Low | Commercial |
| "proposal manager tools" | Low-Medium | Commercial |
| "bid no bid decision framework" | Low | High buyer intent |
| "SAM.gov contract discovery tool" | Very Low | Commercial |

**Action — One Post Per Week:**
- 600–900 words, no fluff
- End every post with: *"BidSmith automates this. See it live → [Book a Demo]"*
- Internal link between posts
- Submit each URL to Google Search Console after publishing

**First 4 posts to write:**
1. *"The Bid/No-Bid Framework That Doubles Your Win Rate"*
2. *"5 Signs Your Proposal Process Is Costing You Contracts"*
3. *"How to Find Government RFPs Before Your Competitors Do"*
4. *"What a Compliance Matrix Should Look Like (And Why Most Don't)"*

## Tier 2 — Medium ROI (Start Week 2–3)

### 4. GovCon Community Presence
**Time:** 30 min/week | **Impact:** Slow burn, high trust

**Communities to join and contribute to:**
- **r/govcontracting** (Reddit) — answer questions, never pitch directly
- **GovCon Wire / GovExec forums** — comment on articles
- **LinkedIn Groups:** "Government Contractors Network", "Federal BD Professionals"
- **Slack:** GovCon communities (search for invites on LinkedIn)

**Playbook:**
1. Find a question about RFPs, proposals, or bid processes
2. Answer it genuinely in 3–5 sentences
3. If directly relevant, add: *"We're building something for exactly this — bidsmith.pro if curious"*

This is not spamming. It's showing up where the ICP already is.

---

### 5. Directory & Review Site Listings
**Time:** 2 hrs one-time | **Impact:** Passive, long-tail

Get listed on platforms where buyers search for tools:

| Platform | Action |
|----------|--------|
| G2.com | Create free listing with category: "Proposal Management Software" |
| Capterra | Submit BidSmith under "Bid Management" |
| Product Hunt | Launch when product is more polished — prep assets now |
| GetApp | Free listing |
| Sourceforge | Free listing |

**Why now:** These take 2–4 weeks to index. Plant the flag early.

---

### 6. Strategic Referral Partner Outreach
**Time:** 1 hr/week | **Impact:** High-quality leads

Who already works with your ICP daily?
- **GovCon consultants** (BD advisors, capture managers for hire)
- **Grant writers** who cross into gov contracts
- **SAM.gov registration services**
- **Small business contracting attorneys**

**Pitch:** *"If you work with companies trying to win more contracts, I'll pay you a referral fee for any demo that converts. Takes 5 mins to refer."*

Start with 10 outreach emails this week to consultants on LinkedIn.

## Tier 3 — Longer Term (Month 2+)

### 7. Paid Search (Google Ads)
**Budget Needed:** $500–$1,000/month to test

**Keywords to bid on:**
- "government bid management software"
- "RFP response tool"
- "proposal management software for government contractors"

**Don't start until:** You have at least 5 demo conversations completed and know your conversion message.

### 8. LinkedIn Ads (Retargeting Only)
Retarget anyone who visited bidsmith.pro but didn't book. Cheap, high-intent.

**Don't start until:** Site gets 200+ monthly visitors organically.
